# Key Findings — Bank Customer Churn Analysis

## 1. Overall Churn Rate
- 2,037 out of 10,000 customers churned = **20.37%**
- Industry benchmark is ~15% — this bank is above average risk

## 2. Germany Is the Problem Market
- Germany churn rate: **32.44%**
- France: 16.15% | Spain: 16.67%
- Germany has 25% of customers but drives 40% of all churn

## 3. Age 46–60 Is the Danger Zone
- Churn rate: **51.12%** — 1 in 2 customers leaving
- Under 30s are most loyal at just 7.56%

## 4. Over-Selling Is Backfiring
- 2 products = **7.58% churn** (sweet spot)
- 4 products = **100% churn** (every single customer left)

## 5. The Bank Is Losing Its Wealthiest Customers
- Churned avg balance: **£91,108**
- Retained avg balance: **£72,745**
- The bank is losing its most valuable, not least valuable, customers

## 6. High-Value Customer Losses
- Top 20 lost customers = **£3.8M+ in deposits**
- All were inactive, mostly with only 1 product

## 7. Engagement Is the Most Controllable Fix
- Inactive churn: **26.85%**
- Active churn: **14.27%**
- Simply re-engaging inactive customers could halve churn risk

## Recommendations
1. Launch a Germany-specific retention campaign immediately
2. Create a re-engagement programme for inactive customers aged 46–60
3. Stop aggressive cross-selling beyond 2 products
4. Assign relationship managers to high-balance inactive accounts
